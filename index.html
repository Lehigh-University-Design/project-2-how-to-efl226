What is Analog Audio?

Vinyl records are analog sound-storage discs made of plastic (polyvinyl chloride). They were the main format for music playback in the 20th century and have seen a recent revival among enthusiasts. The grooves on a record physically encode the music. When a record spins, a stylus (needle) rides in the spiral groove. Each tiny wiggle in the groove corresponds to the original sound wave, and the stylus picks up these vibrations as it moves

Why Should I Care About Vinyl?

Vinyl isn’t just about sound — it’s about ritual. People love the crackle before the first note, the weight of the record in their hands, the artwork you can actually get lost in. Unlike a playlist that disappears into the background, vinyl demands attention: you flip it, you drop the needle, you sit with it. It’s music you don’t just hear, but hold. That tangible, imperfect, all-in experience is why vinyl keeps finding its way back into people’s lives.
